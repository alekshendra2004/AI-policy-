Is 6 months enough? Yes, if you are consistent 5 out of 7 days. Your sociology background plus AI policy is a rare angle. You do not need to compete with CS engineers. You compete as a social scientist who understands governance.
1
Phone in another room during all study blocks. Not silent. Not face-down. Another room. Your brain cannot deep work with a glowing rectangle nearby.
2
The morning 3 are sacred and non-negotiable: Worship, Meditation 1, and Workout. If the day slips, these 3 still happen.
3
No social media before 6 PM. Morning scrolling kills more potential than laziness does.
4
Write something every single day, even 50 words. On AI policy, your day, or your thoughts. Daily reps matter more than perfect output.
5
Sunday is review day, not rest-and-scroll day. 30 minutes of honest review prevents months of drift.
6
No screens after 9:30 PM. Sleep quality directly affects cognitive performance.
7
Track, do not grade. Keep a simple daily log. The goal is pattern recognition, not self-punishment.
8
Apply for at least 3 opportunities per week starting month 3. Studying alone is not enough.
You will miss days. That is built into the plan. Here is what to do in each case:
1
You slept late or had a bad nightShift the schedule forward by 1 hour. Skip the optional evening study. Do not try to catch up by skipping the morning 3.
2
You have to go somewhereBefore you leave, do worship plus meditation 1. That is your minimum viable day. Return to the full schedule the next day.
3
You feel completely unmotivated and do nothingDo 5 minutes of study. If momentum still does not come, do the workout. Movement is often the fastest reset.
4
You miss 2-3 days in a rowDo not double sessions. Return to the base schedule: morning 3 first, then 2 hours of study.
5
You feel burnt outTake one full rest day. Worship, light walk, meals, music. No study. Come back the next day.
6
Golden rule for missed content: Never double up. Never punish. Continue from where you were.
